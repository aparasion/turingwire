Salesforce has announced a significant leap in productivity by migrating its entire development organization to Anthropic's Claude Code, reducing the typical 231-day migration process to just 13 days. This bold claim highlights the potential of AI agents in software development, a topic that is increasingly relevant as companies seek to leverage AI for operational efficiency.

In April 2026, Salesforce reported a remarkable 79 percent increase in pull requests per developer and a five percent decrease in incidents during this migration. However, these figures remain unverified by independent sources, raising questions about the accuracy and replicability of such results. The announcement underscores a growing divide in the tech community regarding the role of AI in coding. Some view this as a transformative shift that could redefine software development, while others caution that it may lead to an accumulation of technical debt, complicating future projects.

As companies consider adopting similar AI-driven solutions, the implications for the market could be profound. If Salesforce's claims hold true, it may prompt a wave of investment in AI tools that promise to streamline development processes. Conversely, skepticism about the sustainability of these gains could lead to a more cautious approach among competitors. 

Looking ahead, it will be crucial to monitor how other organizations respond to Salesforce's claims and whether they can achieve similar results with AI agents.
