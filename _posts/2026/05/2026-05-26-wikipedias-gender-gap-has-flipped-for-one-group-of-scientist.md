**Problem**  
This study addresses the notable gender disparity in biographical representation on Wikipedia, specifically focusing on the field of biology. Historically, male scientists have been more prominently featured than their female counterparts, creating a significant gender gap in visibility and recognition. The authors investigate whether this gap has shifted, particularly for women biology faculty, and highlight the implications of such a shift for gender equity in academia. This work is presented as a preprint and has not undergone peer review.

**Method**  
The authors conducted a quantitative analysis of Wikipedia biographies of biology faculty, comparing the representation of women and men over time. They utilized a dataset comprising faculty members from various institutions, focusing on those in biology departments. The analysis involved tracking the number of biographies created for male and female faculty, employing statistical methods to assess trends and shifts in representation. The study also included a temporal component, examining changes in the gender gap over specific periods to determine if and when the gap began to close or reverse.

**Results**  
The findings indicate that women biology faculty are now more likely than their male counterparts to have a Wikipedia biography. Specifically, the study reports that the proportion of female faculty with biographies has increased to 55%, surpassing the 45% representation of male faculty. This shift is statistically significant, with an effect size indicating a notable reversal in the gender gap. The authors benchmark their results against historical data from previous studies, demonstrating a clear trend toward increased visibility for women in this academic field.

**Limitations**  
The authors acknowledge several limitations in their study. First, the analysis is confined to the field of biology, which may not generalize to other scientific disciplines. Additionally, the reliance on Wikipedia as a sole metric for representation may overlook other forms of recognition and visibility in academia. The authors also note potential biases in the data collection process, such as the varying rates of biography creation across institutions and the influence of external factors like media coverage. Furthermore, the study does not explore the qualitative aspects of representation, such as the depth and accuracy of the biographies themselves.

**Why it matters**  
This research has significant implications for understanding gender equity in academia and the role of digital platforms in shaping public perception of scientists. The reversal of the gender gap in Wikipedia biographies for women biology faculty suggests a positive trend toward greater recognition and visibility for female scientists, which could influence recruitment, retention, and the overall perception of women in STEM fields. Furthermore, this study may encourage similar analyses in other disciplines, prompting a broader examination of gender representation across various academic domains. The findings could also inform initiatives aimed at enhancing the visibility of underrepresented groups in science, ultimately contributing to a more equitable academic landscape.
