Genesis AI, a robotics startup backed by Khosla Ventures, has made significant strides by unveiling its first AI model, GENE-26.5, alongside a demonstration of robotic hands capable of executing intricate tasks. This development is particularly noteworthy as it marks the company’s transition to a full-stack approach in robotics, which could redefine how AI integrates with physical automation.

The $105 million seed funding Genesis AI secured is earmarked for creating foundational AI technologies tailored for robotics. The GENE-26.5 model is designed to enhance the capabilities of robotic systems, enabling them to perform complex operations that were previously challenging. During the demo, the robotic hands showcased their dexterity by manipulating various objects, highlighting the potential for applications in industries such as manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare. This advancement not only positions Genesis AI as a formidable player in the robotics space but also raises the competitive stakes for existing companies in the field.
