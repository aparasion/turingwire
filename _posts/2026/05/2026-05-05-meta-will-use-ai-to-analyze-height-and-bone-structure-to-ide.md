Meta is advancing its efforts to enhance user safety on its platforms by deploying AI technology that analyzes users' height and bone structure to determine if they are underage. This initiative, currently in operation in select countries, aims to create a more secure online environment, particularly for younger users, and reflects the company's commitment to addressing concerns about age verification in the digital space.

The AI system leverages advanced visual analysis to assess physical characteristics that may indicate a user's age, a move that comes amid increasing scrutiny over how social media platforms manage underage access. While specific countries have begun testing this technology, Meta plans to expand its rollout, potentially impacting millions of users globally. The company has not disclosed the exact number of users affected or the countries involved, but the implications are significant. If successful, this system could set a new standard for age verification, compelling competitors to adopt similar measures or risk falling behind in user safety protocols.

As Meta continues to refine this technology, it raises questions about privacy and the ethical implications of using AI for such personal assessments. Users and advocates alike will be watching closely to see how this technology evolves and whether it effectively balances safety with individual rights. The next steps will likely involve public feedback and regulatory scrutiny, as the industry grapples with the challenges of ensuring a safe online environment for all users.
