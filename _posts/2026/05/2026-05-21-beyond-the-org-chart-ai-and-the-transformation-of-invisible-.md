**Problem**  
This preprint addresses the gap in understanding how AI adoption is transforming work processes and professional interactions within organizations, particularly in product-focused roles. While existing literature has explored the technical implications of AI, there is limited insight into its impact on informal cultural practices, such as mentoring and collaboration, which are crucial for career development and employee engagement.

**Method**  
The authors conducted qualitative interviews with 24 product-focused professionals at a large technology firm. The interviews aimed to gather in-depth insights into how AI has influenced their work responsibilities, team dynamics, and professional relationships. The analysis focused on identifying both positive and negative changes resulting from AI integration, particularly regarding role responsibilities and informal cultural practices. The authors also propose actionable steps for organizations to enhance visibility of "invisible work" and maintain healthy workplace cultures amidst AI transformation.

**Results**  
The findings indicate that AI is reshaping formal role responsibilities and enhancing collaboration among peers, leading to smoother workflows. However, the authors also highlight significant risks to traditional career growth opportunities, such as mentorship and feedback mechanisms, which are essential for professional development. The qualitative nature of the study does not provide quantitative metrics or comparisons against specific baselines or benchmarks, but the insights suggest a nuanced understanding of the dual impact of AI on workplace dynamics.

**Limitations**  
The authors acknowledge that the study's sample size is limited to 24 individuals from a single organization, which may not be representative of broader industry trends. Additionally, the qualitative methodology may introduce biases based on individual experiences and perceptions. The paper does not quantify the effect sizes of the observed changes, nor does it explore the long-term implications of these transformations on employee retention and satisfaction.

**Why it matters**  
This research has significant implications for organizations adopting AI technologies. By highlighting the potential risks to informal cultural practices and career development, the paper calls for a reevaluation of how AI is integrated into workplace processes. The proposed steps for making invisible work more visible can help organizations foster a supportive environment that encourages diverse thinking and collaboration. This work lays the groundwork for future studies to quantitatively assess the impact of AI on workplace dynamics and to develop frameworks for mitigating the risks identified.
