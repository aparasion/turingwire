NVIDIA has introduced the T3000 and T2000 modules, based on the NVIDIA Thor architecture, aimed at advancing robotics and edge AI. These modules are designed to meet the growing demand for compact, power-efficient AI supercomputers capable of running foundation models at the edge. NVIDIA stated that general-purpose robots and autonomous machines are transitioning from research labs to mass-market deployment. This launch follows NVIDIA's recent shift away from Nvidia for custom chips, indicating a broader trend in AI infrastructure development. The introduction of these modules could enable developers to build more capable and efficient robotic systems, enhancing the deployment of AI in real-world applications.
