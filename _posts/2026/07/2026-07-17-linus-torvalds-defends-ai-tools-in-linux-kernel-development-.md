Linus Torvalds, the creator of Linux, has publicly endorsed the use of AI tools in kernel development, specifically mentioning the AI-powered code review tool Sashiko from the Linux Foundation. He stated, "Linux is not one of those anti-AI projects," and emphasized that he would "very loudly ignore" anyone who attempts to dissuade others from utilizing AI technologies. This stance reinforces the Linux Foundation's commitment to integrating AI into its development processes, marking a significant shift in the community's approach to AI tools. Torvalds' comments come amidst ongoing discussions about the role of AI in software development, following previous coverage of the Linux Foundation's initiatives to address open-source vulnerabilities. For practitioners, this indicates a growing acceptance and potential integration of AI tools in mainstream Linux development workflows, as endorsed by key figures in the community.
