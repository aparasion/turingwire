A coalition of 200 economists and AI researchers, including 16 Nobel laureates, has issued a statement highlighting the urgent need to prepare for AI's economic impact. They assert that the AI transformation could surpass the Industrial Revolution but unfold in a fraction of the time. This warning involves major players in the AI field, including Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic. The rapid pace of AI development necessitates immediate action to mitigate potential economic disruptions. This follows previous discussions on AI's job creation potential, emphasizing the need for proactive measures in workforce adaptation. For practitioners, this underscores the importance of integrating AI strategies into economic planning to stay ahead of the impending changes, as reported by [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/nobel-laureates-and-ai-leaders-warn-the-window-to-prepare-for-ais-economic-impact-is-closing-fast/).
