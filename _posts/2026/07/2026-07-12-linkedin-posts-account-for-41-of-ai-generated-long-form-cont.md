41% of long-form posts on LinkedIn have been flagged as AI-written, according to a recent study by Pangram analyzing social media content across five platforms. The study found that one in four longer social media posts were entirely AI-generated, with LinkedIn accounting for one third of all posts scanned. Notably, nearly two-thirds of the detected AI content originated from LinkedIn. The detection model used in the study was noted to flag content conservatively, suggesting that the actual rate of AI-generated content could be even higher. This finding highlights LinkedIn's dominance in the long-form AI content space, which may influence how AI-generated content is perceived and managed on social media. For practitioners, this underscores the need for robust content verification mechanisms on platforms like LinkedIn to address the growing prevalence of AI-generated material.
