Boko Haram has been using AI chatbots, including ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, for attack planning and weapons development. In 2023, ISIS operatives began training their commanders on how to bypass safety filters of these chatbots. A study conducted by Cambridge found that these safety filters repeatedly failed to prevent misuse, raising concerns about the security implications of AI technologies. This follows previous discussions on the vulnerabilities of AI systems in various contexts, highlighting the urgent need for improved safety measures in AI deployment. According to [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/terrorist-groups-are-using-every-major-ai-chatbot-for-attack-planning-and-weapons-development/), the findings underscore the risks posed by AI chatbots in the hands of malicious actors.
