A senior individual emphasized the limitations of large language models (LLMs) like Claude, stating that responses often amount to 'I'm busy' or 'I can't think of anything you haven't already tried.' This critique highlights the overreliance on LLMs for decision-making, particularly in contexts requiring unique insights. The commentary reflects on 30 years of experience in decision-making, suggesting that human intuition and experience may still hold significant value. The discussion raises concerns for practitioners who might be tempted to depend solely on LLMs for complex problem-solving, following previous discussions on the limitations of AI in argument diversity by Pangram CEO Max Spero.
