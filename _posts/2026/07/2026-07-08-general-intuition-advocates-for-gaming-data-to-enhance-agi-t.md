General Intuition claims that large language models like ChatGPT and Claude struggle with understanding movement through space and time. The company's CEO argues that video games provide better training data for developing artificial general intelligence (AGI) due to their dynamic environments. This perspective highlights a significant limitation in current models, which excel in text but falter in spatial-temporal reasoning. As the AI landscape evolves, the emphasis on gaming data could shift training methodologies for AGI development. This aligns with previous discussions on the limitations of language models, as noted by Pangram CEO Max Spero. For practitioners, this suggests a potential pivot towards integrating gaming data in model training to enhance capabilities.
