Microsoft is expanding its Azure AI infrastructure by adopting AMD's Helios platform, which is expected to challenge Nvidia's GPU systems in the second half of 2026. This shift indicates a strategic move away from Nvidia's dominance in the AI chip market. Additionally, Anthropic is testing AMD hardware, suggesting a potential trend among AI companies to diversify their hardware sources. The adoption of AMD's Helios platform could provide practitioners with more options for AI infrastructure, enhancing competition in the market. This follows previous reports of Microsoft's significant investments in AI capabilities and infrastructure, further solidifying its commitment to diversifying its AI technology stack, as noted by [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/nvidias-grip-on-ai-chips-weakens-as-microsoft-turns-to-amd-and-anthropic-may-follow/).
