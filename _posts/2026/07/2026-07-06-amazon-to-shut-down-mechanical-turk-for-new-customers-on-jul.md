On July 30, 2026, Amazon Web Services will officially shut down its crowdsourcing service, Mechanical Turk, for new customers. This decision marks the end of a platform that has been a significant player in the AI training data landscape, often referred to as the original 'Artificial Artificial Intelligence.' Existing customers will still have access to the service, but this move signals a shift in Amazon's strategy regarding crowdsourced data solutions. The implications for AI practitioners include the need to seek alternative platforms for human-in-the-loop tasks, as Mechanical Turk has been a staple resource for many in the industry. This follows a broader trend of companies reevaluating their reliance on traditional crowdsourcing methods in favor of more automated solutions.
