OpenAI and Work Louder have announced the release of Codex Micro, a compact hardware controller designed specifically for interacting with AI agents. This product aims to replace traditional command typing with joystick-based control, enhancing user experience and efficiency. The shift to joystick control reflects OpenAI's strategy to streamline AI interactions, potentially impacting how developers design user interfaces for AI applications. This follows OpenAI's recent focus on improving AI safety and robustness with the launch of GPT-Red. For developers, the introduction of Codex Micro may change the way they build and integrate AI functionalities into their products, as they adapt to new input modalities. More details can be found in [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/openai-wants-developers-to-stop-typing-commands-and-start-using-a-joystick-to-control-their-ai-agents/).
