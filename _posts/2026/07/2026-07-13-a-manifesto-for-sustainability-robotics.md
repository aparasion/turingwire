## Problem
The paper addresses the fragmentation in the field of robotics concerning sustainability efforts, proposing a cohesive framework to enhance societal and environmental impacts. It highlights the need for a dedicated discipline, termed Sustainability Robotics, to unify various approaches and methodologies that currently exist in isolation. This work is presented as a preprint, indicating that it has not yet undergone peer review.

## Method
The authors propose three guiding principles for Sustainability Robotics, which serve as foundational tenets for the discipline. Additionally, they outline two dimensions that encompass sustainable design and the application of robotics for sustainability. While specific architectures, loss functions, or training methodologies are not detailed, the framework aims to integrate existing technologies and practices under a common goal of sustainability.

## Limitations
The authors acknowledge the nascent stage of the proposed discipline and the potential challenges in achieving consensus on the guiding principles and dimensions. They do not address specific technical limitations or empirical validations of the proposed framework, which may hinder immediate application in practical scenarios.

## Why it matters
The establishment of Sustainability Robotics could significantly influence future research and development in robotics, promoting interdisciplinary collaboration and innovation aimed at addressing pressing environmental challenges.
