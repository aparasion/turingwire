Meta laid off 8,000 employees, with a lawsuit claiming the company used internal AI systems to generate the layoff lists. This lawsuit is filed in a California federal court, alleging discriminatory practices in the selection process. The claims suggest that the AI-driven mass layoffs may have disproportionately affected certain groups, raising legal and ethical concerns about the use of AI in employment decisions. This follows ongoing scrutiny of Meta's AI practices, including recent reports on the slower-than-expected progress of its AI agents, as noted by Mark Zuckerberg. The implications of this lawsuit could influence how companies implement AI in workforce management, particularly regarding transparency and fairness.
