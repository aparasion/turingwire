Euwyn Poon, known for his role in building 250,000 e-scooters at Spin, has successfully raised $5 million to fund his ambitious new venture, Orbital. This initiative aims to establish 10,000 space data centers, a move that signifies a notable pivot from the transportation sector to the burgeoning field of space technology. The timing is particularly relevant as the demand for data processing and storage capabilities continues to surge, driven by advancements in satellite technology and the increasing reliance on cloud services.

Poon's vision for Orbital is to create a network of space-based data centers that can leverage the unique advantages of operating outside Earth's atmosphere. By positioning data centers in space, Poon believes he can address issues related to latency and bandwidth that terrestrial data centers often face. The $5 million raised will be instrumental in developing the necessary technology and infrastructure to support this ambitious project. As [TechCrunch AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/09/how-an-e-scooter-founder-raised-5-million-to-build-space-data-centers/) reported, this funding round highlights the growing interest in space-based solutions among investors, particularly as the industry matures.

The competitive landscape for space data centers is still in its infancy, but several companies are beginning to explore similar concepts. Poon's approach may set him apart, especially given his background in scaling operations at Spin. The potential for Orbital to provide faster and more efficient data processing could attract clients from various sectors, including telecommunications, finance, and even entertainment, all of which are increasingly reliant on data analytics and storage solutions. The projected establishment of 10,000 space data centers could significantly alter the market dynamics, positioning Orbital as a key player in the future of data management.

As the space industry continues to evolve, the implications of Poon's venture extend beyond just data centers. The initiative could pave the way for new business models and partnerships, particularly with satellite operators and tech companies looking to enhance their data capabilities. The success of this project could also inspire further investments in space technology, potentially leading to a new wave of innovation in the sector.

Looking ahead, the next steps for Orbital will be crucial, particularly in terms of technology development and securing additional funding to scale operations.
