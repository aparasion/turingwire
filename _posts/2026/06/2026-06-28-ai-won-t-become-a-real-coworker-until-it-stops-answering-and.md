A recent survey paper authored by researchers from Tencent and several Chinese universities explores the evolution of AI from simple chatbots to potential 'digital colleagues.' The authors argue that current AI systems are insufficient as reliable coworkers because they primarily generate answers rather than completing entire tasks within persistent work environments. This limitation hinders their effectiveness in collaborative settings.

The researchers emphasize that the key to advancing AI capabilities lies in the integration of persistent workspaces and the development of reusable skills. By focusing on these aspects, AI could transition from merely responding to queries to actively participating in task completion, thereby enhancing its utility in professional contexts. The findings suggest that for AI to be considered a true coworker, it must evolve beyond its current capabilities of answering questions to executing and finishing tasks autonomously. This perspective is crucial for future developments in AI applications in workplace environments.
