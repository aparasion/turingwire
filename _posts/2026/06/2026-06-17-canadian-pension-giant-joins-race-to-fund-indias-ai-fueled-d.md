This investment underscores the increasing global interest in India's burgeoning AI sector, particularly as demand for data storage and processing capabilities surges. The timing is crucial as businesses worldwide seek robust infrastructure to support their AI initiatives.

CtrlS operates over 15 data centers across India, positioning itself as a key player in the region's data management landscape. The investment from the Canadian pension giant is expected to bolster CtrlS's capabilities and expand its reach, enhancing the overall data center ecosystem in India. As organizations increasingly rely on AI technologies, the need for reliable and scalable data centers becomes paramount, making this investment particularly timely.
