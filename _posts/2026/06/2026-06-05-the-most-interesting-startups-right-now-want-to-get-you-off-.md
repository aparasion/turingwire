In a notable shift within the tech landscape, several innovative startups are emerging with the goal of encouraging users to disconnect from their phones and engage more in real-world interactions. Founders like Brynn Putnam, known for her work with Mirror, are spearheading initiatives that prioritize in-person games and social experiences. This trend is particularly relevant as society grapples with the implications of excessive screen time, making it a timely conversation in the tech community.

Among the startups making waves is Board, which focuses on creating engaging in-person games designed to foster social connections. Their approach reflects a growing recognition of the need for balance in a digital-first world. Another noteworthy player is Cyberdeck, which specializes in DIY computers, appealing to a niche audience that values hands-on experiences over passive consumption. This resurgence of interest in tangible, offline activities signals a potential pivot in consumer behavior, as more individuals seek meaningful interactions beyond their screens.

The context for these startups is underscored by the ongoing boom in AI fundraising, which continues to break records. As reported by [TechCrunch AI](https://techcrunch.com/video/the-most-interesting-startups-right-now-want-to-get-you-off-your-phone/), the AI sector has seen unprecedented investment levels, suggesting that while many companies are focused on digital solutions, there is also a counter-movement emphasizing human connection. This duality presents both opportunities and challenges for startups like Board and Cyberdeck, which must navigate a competitive landscape dominated by tech giants and their digital offerings.

The implications for users are significant. As these startups gain traction, they may reshape how people interact with technology and each other. The push for in-person engagement could lead to a cultural shift, encouraging users to prioritize face-to-face connections over virtual ones. For investors, this trend presents a unique opportunity to support ventures that align with emerging consumer preferences, potentially leading to lucrative returns as the market evolves.

Looking ahead, it will be crucial to monitor how these startups adapt to the competitive pressures of the tech industry and whether their focus on reducing phone dependency resonates with a broader audience.
