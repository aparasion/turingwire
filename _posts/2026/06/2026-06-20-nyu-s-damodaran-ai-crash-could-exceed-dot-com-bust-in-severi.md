Aswath Damodaran, a finance professor at NYU, asserts that a potential AI crash could be more painful than the bursting of the dot-com bubble. He highlights that massive amounts of debt-financed infrastructure are being built within the AI industry, raising concerns about sustainability. Damodaran emphasizes that the prevailing business model aims to replace entire jobs, leading to unclear societal consequences. This commentary follows ongoing discussions about the economic implications of AI systems, including warnings from Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella about a small number of AI systems capturing all economic returns. Practitioners should be aware of the risks associated with over-reliance on AI technologies, as articulated by Damodaran's analysis.
