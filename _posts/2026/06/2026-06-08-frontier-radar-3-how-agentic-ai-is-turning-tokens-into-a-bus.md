The third edition of Frontier Radar highlights a significant shift in the AI landscape, focusing on the rise of agentic AI and its implications for business metrics. This evolution is particularly relevant as companies increasingly transition from traditional subscription models to consumption-based billing, driven by the growing demand for more efficient and specialized AI workflows.

Agentic AI, which refers to systems that can autonomously perform tasks and make decisions, is consuming "many times more tokens" than previous models. This surge in token consumption is prompting businesses to rethink how they measure the value of AI. As noted by [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/frontier-radar-3-how-agentic-ai-is-turning-tokens-into-a-business-metric/), the billing model is shifting from a flat-rate subscription to a consumption-based approach, where costs are tied directly to the number of tokens used. This change reflects a broader trend in the industry, where companies are looking for more granular metrics to assess the economic value generated by AI systems.

The report emphasizes that while token consumption is a critical factor, it should not be the sole measure of AI value creation. One source quoted in the report argues that "token prices vary according to speed, specialization, and the economic value of the result," indicating that businesses must consider multiple dimensions of performance when evaluating AI systems. This nuanced understanding could lead to more strategic investments in AI technologies, as companies seek to optimize their workflows and maximize returns.

Competitively, this shift may impact how AI providers position their offerings. As consumption-based models gain traction, companies that can demonstrate superior efficiency and value through their agentic AI solutions may have a distinct advantage. This could lead to a more competitive marketplace, where firms are incentivized to innovate and enhance their AI capabilities to attract clients.

As the AI landscape continues to evolve, stakeholders should keep an eye on how these billing model changes will affect user experiences and market dynamics. The growing emphasis on consumption metrics could redefine how businesses approach AI investments and operational strategies in the coming months.
