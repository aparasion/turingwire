OpenAI is developing a custom inference chip named Jalapeño in partnership with Broadcom. This move is part of a broader trend where companies like SpaceX, Google, and Apple are also building their own chips to reduce reliance on Nvidia, which has dominated the AI chip market for years. The shift aims to mitigate single-supplier risk, indicating a potential end to total dependence on Nvidia for AI chips. This follows previous developments in the industry, including OpenAI's collaboration with Broadcom on the Jalapeño chip. According to [TechCrunch AI](https://techcrunch.com/video/why-everyone-from-openai-to-spacex-is-building-their-own-chips-and-turning-up-the-heat-on-nvidia/), this trend reflects a significant change in how major tech companies approach chip production.
