The Linux Foundation has launched the Akrites initiative, involving 20 tech companies, AI labs, and banks, aimed at fixing vulnerabilities in critical open-source software. This collaboration seeks to address security flaws before they can be exploited by AI tools. Participants include major players in the tech industry, reflecting a concerted effort to enhance the security of open-source projects. This follows increasing concerns about the potential for AI-powered attacks on software vulnerabilities, underscoring the urgency of the initiative. The initiative is a proactive measure to safeguard the integrity of open-source software, which is foundational to many AI applications, as reported by [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/linux-foundation-and-20-tech-giants-launch-akrites-to-fix-open-source-flaws-before-ai-powered-attacks-hit/).
