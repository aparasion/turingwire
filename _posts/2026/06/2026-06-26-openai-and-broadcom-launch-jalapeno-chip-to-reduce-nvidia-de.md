OpenAI has unveiled its custom inference chip, Jalapeño, developed in collaboration with Broadcom. This move comes as major tech companies, including Google, Apple, and SpaceX, are also designing their own AI chips to mitigate reliance on Nvidia, which has historically dominated the AI chip market. OpenAI's initiative reflects a broader industry trend towards reducing single-supplier risk in AI hardware. The Jalapeño chip aims to enhance performance and efficiency for AI applications, marking a significant step in the ongoing shift away from Nvidia's monopoly in the sector. This follows a series of developments where companies are increasingly investing in proprietary chip designs to address supply chain vulnerabilities, as reported by [TechCrunch AI](https://techcrunch.com/podcast/openais-jalapeno-chip-is-big-techs-spiciest-move-away-from-nvidia/).
