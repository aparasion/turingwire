JUPITER, Europe’s first exascale supercomputer, was unveiled during ISC week in Hamburg. Located at Forschungszentrum Jülich, JUPITER utilizes NVIDIA Grace Hopper Superchips for processing and NVIDIA Quantum-X800 InfiniBand for networking. Four projects currently running on JUPITER demonstrate its capabilities, including initiatives aimed at mapping the human brain. This follows NVIDIA's ongoing efforts to advance supercomputing capabilities in Europe, as seen in previous announcements regarding AI infrastructure and partnerships. The introduction of JUPITER signifies a significant leap in computational power available for scientific research and complex simulations, impacting various fields reliant on high-performance computing. More details are available on the [NVIDIA Blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/jupiter-exascale-supercomputing-science/).
