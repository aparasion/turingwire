Norway will implement a ban on generative AI tools in elementary schools, affecting grades 1 through 7, starting in late August. Prime Minister Stoere emphasized the need for children to learn foundational skills, stating, "Children must first learn to read, write, and do math." While generative AI usage will be prohibited in elementary education, it will still be permitted in secondary schools under supervision. This decision reflects ongoing concerns about the impact of AI on early learning, following a broader trend of educational institutions reevaluating technology's role in the classroom. For practitioners in educational technology, this ban may limit the deployment of generative AI solutions in early education settings.
