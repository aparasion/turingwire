Huawei has achieved a notable milestone in its quest for AI independence by successfully completing the post-training of its DeepSeek-V4-Pro model using the company's Ascend 910C chip, instead of relying on NVIDIA's technology. This development is particularly significant as it underscores China's growing capabilities in AI, a sector where the country has been striving to reduce its dependence on foreign technology.

The successful training of DeepSeek-V4-Pro on Huawei's proprietary chip marks a pivotal moment in the ongoing race for technological supremacy in AI. The Ascend 910C chip is part of Huawei's broader strategy to enhance its AI infrastructure and capabilities, which have been hampered by U.S. sanctions limiting access to advanced semiconductor technologies. This achievement not only showcases Huawei's engineering prowess but also reflects China's ambitions to establish itself as a leader in the global AI landscape.

In the competitive context, this move positions Huawei as a direct challenger to NVIDIA, which has long dominated the AI hardware market with its GPUs. The shift to using the Ascend 910C could potentially alter the dynamics of AI model training, as companies may start to consider alternatives to NVIDIA's offerings.
