OpenAI's CEO Sam Altman has stated that the company will not pursue an IPO unless it can achieve a valuation of at least $1 trillion. Advisors have reportedly recommended that OpenAI delay its public offering until 2027. This decision comes in the wake of a significant 13 percent drop in SoftBank's stock value in a single day, which could impact investor sentiment and market conditions. The delay reflects a strategic choice to optimize valuation rather than rush to market, potentially affecting how AI practitioners view investment opportunities in the sector. This follows previous discussions about reducing dependence on Nvidia, as seen in OpenAI's recent collaborations with Broadcom on custom chips.
