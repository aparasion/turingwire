Anthropic is significantly scaling its Project Glasswing initiative, which focuses on identifying and mitigating security vulnerabilities, by extending access to its AI model, Mythos, to 150 organizations across 15 countries. This move is particularly crucial as it targets critical infrastructure sectors such as power, water, healthcare, and communications—areas where a cyberattack could potentially impact up to 100 million people. The urgency of this expansion comes amid rising concerns over cybersecurity threats globally.

The expansion of Project Glasswing is a strategic response to the increasing frequency and sophistication of cyberattacks on essential services. By integrating Mythos into these organizations, Anthropic aims to enhance their resilience against potential threats. This initiative not only underscores the growing importance of AI in cybersecurity but also positions Anthropic as a key player in safeguarding critical infrastructure. As noted by [TechCrunch AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/anthropic-scales-claude-mythos-to-critical-infrastructure-in-15-countries/), the program's reach now extends to regions where the consequences of cyber incidents could be catastrophic.

Key sectors benefiting from this initiative include utilities and healthcare, where operational disruptions can lead to severe public safety risks. For instance, a cyberattack on a water supply system could compromise clean water access for millions, while disruptions in healthcare systems could delay critical medical services. By providing these organizations with advanced AI tools, Anthropic is not only enhancing their security posture but also fostering greater public trust in the safety of essential services.

The competitive landscape is also shifting as other AI firms and cybersecurity companies may feel pressured to enhance their offerings in response to Anthropic's bold move. Companies like OpenAI and Google are likely to ramp up their own security initiatives to maintain their positions in this rapidly evolving market. The integration of AI into cybersecurity is becoming a necessity rather than a luxury, and organizations that fail to adapt may find themselves vulnerable to attacks.

Looking ahead, the focus will be on how effectively these organizations implement Mythos and the tangible improvements in their security measures. The success of Project Glasswing could set a precedent for future collaborations between AI companies and critical infrastructure sectors, potentially reshaping the landscape of cybersecurity.
