The initiative, named "Raise Us," has secured $1 billion in funding from Amazon, Microsoft, Anthropic, and the OpenAI Foundation to prepare American workers for job shifts driven by AI automation. Launched by former US Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo, the program aims to address the workforce disruptions caused by the very companies financing it, raising potential questions about its independence. This follows a growing trend of tech companies acknowledging their role in workforce changes, as seen in previous discussions about AI's impact on retail and other sectors. Practitioners in workforce development and AI policy may need to consider the implications of corporate-funded retraining programs on their strategies.
