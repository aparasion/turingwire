**Problem**  
The paper addresses the gap in effective management of gestational diabetes, particularly in resource-constrained settings, by proposing a digital solution that integrates patient-generated data with clinical workflows. Existing literature lacks comprehensive systems that facilitate continuous monitoring and individualized care outside traditional clinical environments. This work is a preprint and has not undergone peer review.

**Method**  
CARE-link employs a modular architecture that integrates a large language model (LLM) to mediate interactions between patients and clinicians. The platform aggregates data from patients, summarizes clinical information, and provides context-aware decision support. It utilizes a WhatsApp interface for patient communication, delivering management plans and lifestyle guidance. The system is designed to be adaptable for other chronic conditions requiring similar longitudinal tracking and behavioral support. Specifics regarding the LLM architecture, training data, or compute resources are not disclosed in the paper.

**Results**  
While the paper does not provide quantitative performance metrics or comparisons against established baselines, it emphasizes qualitative improvements in patient engagement and clinician oversight. The authors report enhanced patient compliance and continuity of care as key outcomes, particularly in settings where in-person follow-ups are challenging. The effectiveness of CARE-link in real-world applications remains to be validated through empirical studies.

**Limitations**  
The authors acknowledge that the platform's success is contingent on user adoption and the digital literacy of both patients and clinicians. They also note potential challenges in data privacy and security, given the sensitive nature of health information. Additionally, the lack of empirical validation and performance metrics against existing diabetes management systems is a significant limitation that could affect the perceived efficacy of CARE-link.

**Why it matters**  
CARE-link represents a significant step towards integrating digital health solutions into chronic disease management, particularly for gestational diabetes. Its dual-facing design not only enhances clinical oversight but also empowers patients through accessible communication channels. The implications of this work extend to the broader field of digital health, suggesting that similar frameworks could be developed for other chronic conditions, thereby improving patient outcomes and healthcare efficiency. This aligns with ongoing trends in telemedicine and digital health interventions, as discussed in related literature (as published in [arXiv cs.CL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04952v1)).
