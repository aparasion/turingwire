**Problem**  
This paper addresses a significant policy shift by the European Research Council (ERC) regarding the resubmission of research proposals. The abrupt rule change aimed to restrict the ability of researchers to resubmit proposals that had previously been rejected. This decision faced substantial backlash from the scientific community, as evidenced by an open letter signed by over 1,000 scientists. The paper serves as a commentary on the implications of such policy changes for research funding and the broader scientific ecosystem. It is important to note that this work is a preprint and has not undergone peer review.

The primary outcome of the paper is the ERC's reversal of the proposed rule change, which was influenced by the significant outcry from the scientific community.
